Wireframes

The bridge between product requirements and engineering implementation

My Wireframing Approach

Wireframing isn't just about drawing boxesit's about translating complex product requirements into visual specifications that engineering teams can build from.

I focus on clarity, completeness, and actionability. Every wireframe I create answers the question: "Can an engineer build this without asking questions?" My deliverables include not just the screens, but also the states, edge cases, and documentation needed for complete implementation.

Fidelity Progression

From initial concept to production-ready specifications

Low-Fidelity

30 min - 2 hours

Quick concept exploration focusing on layout, information hierarchy, and user flow without visual design distractions.

  • Rapid ideation
  • Layout exploration
  • Stakeholder alignment
  • Early feedback loops

Mid-Fidelity

2 - 4 hours

Refined structure with component definitions, spacing systems, and interaction patterns for engineering review.

  • Component structure
  • Spacing & alignment
  • Interaction states
  • Content hierarchy

High-Fidelity

4 - 8 hours

Complete visual designs with interactions, animations, and realistic content for usability testing and final handoff.

  • Visual design system
  • Interactive prototypes
  • Usability testing
  • Developer handoff
